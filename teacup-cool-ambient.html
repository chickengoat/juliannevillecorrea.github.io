Tea Cup Cooling Problem

Question:

How long does it take for a tea cup with a curved surface area of 35 cm², open to air on the top, to cool from 90°C to 30°C in a room at 20°C? Assume the heat transfer coefficient for natural convection in air is 10 W/m²K. Assume the tea has a mass of 200g, and the specific heat capacity of water is 4186 J/kg·K. The top of the tea cup is circular with a radius of 4 cm.

Solution:

Step 1: Energy Loss Calculation (via Newton’s Law of Cooling)

The total surface area of the tea cup exposed to air is the sum of the curved surface area and the top surface area, which is given by:

\[ A_{\text{total}} = A + A_{\text{top}} = 0.0035 + \pi r^2 = 0.0035 + 0.00503 = 0.00853 \, \text{m}^2 \]

According to Newton's Law of Cooling, the rate of heat loss is:

\[ q = h A_{\text{total}} (T_{\text{tea}} - T_{\text{room}}) \]

Substituting the known values:

\[ q = 10 \cdot 0.00853 \cdot (T_{\text{tea}} - 20) = 0.0853 \cdot (T_{\text{tea}} - 20) \, \text{W} \]

Step 2: Heat Capacity of the Tea

The heat energy contained in the tea is given by:

\[ Q = mc(T_{\text{tea}} - T_{\text{room}}) \]

Substituting the values for the mass \(m = 0.2 \, \text{kg}\), specific heat capacity \(c = 4186 \, \text{J/kg} \cdot \text{K}\), and initial temperature \(T_{\text{tea}} = 90^\circ C\), we get:

\[ Q = 0.2 \cdot 4186 \cdot (90 - 20) = 58604 \, \text{J} \]

Step 3: Cooling Time Calculation

The rate of change of temperature is governed by:

\[ \frac{dT}{dt} = - \frac{h A_{\text{total}}}{mc} (T - T_{\text{room}}) \]

Substituting the values:

\[ \frac{dT}{dt} = - \frac{10 \times 0.00853}{0.2 \times 4186} (T - 20) = -0.0000102 (T - 20) \]

Step 4: Solving the Differential Equation

The solution to the differential equation is:

\[ T(t) = T_{\text{room}} + (T_0 - T_{\text{room}}) e^{-kt} \]

Where \(k = 0.0000102 \, \text{s}^{-1}\), and the initial temperature \(T_0 = 90^\circ C\). Therefore:

\[ T(t) = 20 + 70 e^{-0.0000102 t} \]

Step 5: Finding the Time to Cool

We want to find the time \(t\) when the temperature reaches 30°C. Solving for \(t\):

\[ 30 = 20 + 70 e^{-0.0000102 t} \]

Solving for \(t\):

\[ e^{-0.0000102 t} = \frac{1}{7} \] \[ -0.0000102 t = \ln{\frac{1}{7}} = -1.9459 \] \[ t = \frac{1.9459}{0.0000102} = 190775 \, \text{seconds} \approx 53 \, \text{hours} \]

Conclusion

It will take approximately 53 hours for the tea to cool from 90°C to 30°C under the given assumptions.
